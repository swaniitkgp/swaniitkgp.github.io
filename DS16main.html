Drone Squad 16

A SWAN Lab, CSE, IIT Kharagpur Initiative

The drone group at SWAN lab works in the domain of networking, communication and control of UAVs (currently quadrotors/quadcopters) with an aim to address various real-time issues plaguing several domains such as – Military, Disaster management, Agriculture, Infrastructure (communication link setup, connectivity), traffic and others. Various avenues and solutions are being actively explored for arming the UAVs with offensive, defensive as well as peace-time application capabilities. Some of these application areas are actively supported by projects from various Government departments while the remaining ones are taken up on a self-interest basis. Application areas such as agriculture are actively supported by various governmental agencies, whereas efforts are underway to involve various agencies and ministries for areas involving military and disaster management based applications.

Technologically, the group focuses more on the networking and communication aspects of UAVs as compared to its structural and energy aspects. Deep learning based data prediction and tracking, Reinforcement learning based stabilization and control, networked controls (both aerial networking as well as air-ground networking), wireless UAV network penetration testing and others are some of the technological aspects being worked upon. From a research point of view, the group’s works can be summarized into the following six areas – automation, swarm, localization, networks, security vulnerabilities and tracking. These problems are being handled by a group of PhD scholars, Project staff, Masters and Undergraduate students under my guidance.
